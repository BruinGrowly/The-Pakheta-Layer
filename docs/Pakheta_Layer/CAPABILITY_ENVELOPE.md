## 1. Purpose

This pass asks whether Pakheta is being too narrowly framed as an audit or governance layer.

The experiment treats Pakheta as a broader relational capability space and probes seven functions:

```text
generative field design
blind primitive discovery
prediction before repair
field composition
boundary and falsification
emergent primitive search
creative actualization
```

The goal is not to prove that Pakheta can do everything. The goal is to map where it behaves like a real relational layer, where it abstains, and where its current model reaches a limit.

Working principle:

```text
Pakheta is not only governance over relation.
Pakheta is the capability layer by which relation can be detected, shaped, repaired, composed, translated, bounded, and actualized.
```

## 11. Limits Observed

The experiment preserves several limits:

- fragmented fields remain fragmented rather than being forced into coherence
- boundary cases can and should abstain
- latent-ready fields can still require Love before Power
- emergent primitive search proposes candidates rather than declaring new primitives
- current results are synthetic and should be stress-tested with broader generators
